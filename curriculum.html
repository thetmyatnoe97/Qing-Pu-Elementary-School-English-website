International Education

International Education Framework

Aligned with the Ministry of Education's White Paper on International Education 2.0, our programme cultivates multicultural awareness and global citizenship through four orientations and twelve competency indicators.

Design Philosophy — L.I.O.I.A.

Our curriculum extends from the school's vision through five design principles:

  • L — Living Life: Grounding every lesson in daily experience
  • I — Interesting: Making global topics fun and engaging
  • O — Operating: Learning by doing, not just listening
  • I — Immersive: Deep cultural immersion in each theme
  • A — Achievement: Building real confidence through success

From 2019, the curriculum introduces PBL (Project-Based Learning) with mind-map theme structures. From 2021, a Science theme was added for Grades 3–6.

Four Orientations of International Education

Based on Ministry of Education International Education 2.0 — elementary school stage competency indicators.

01

Demonstrate National Value

Understand Taiwan's characteristics in the global context, cultural contributions, and international competitiveness — building self-esteem and confidence in our own culture.

  • National E1 — Cultural characteristics of my country vs. the world
  • National E2 — Local identity with an international perspective
  • National E3 — Express native culture characteristics
02

Respect Multiculturalism & International Understanding

Recognise the diversity of international cultures — from understanding and respecting to appreciating different values, and putting world peace into action.

  • National E4 — Understand international cultural diversity
  • National E5 — Develop willingness to learn different cultures
  • National E6 — Distinguish characteristics of conflict and peace
03

Strengthen International Mobility

Develop foreign language skills, inquiry and critical thinking, information technology use, and cross-cultural communication abilities.

  • National E7 — Recognise various international capabilities
  • National E8 — Understand importance of international competence
  • National E9 — Participate in international cultural activities
04

Be a Responsible Global Citizen

Recognise basic human rights and moral responsibilities, understand globally disadvantaged situations, and implement sustainable development in daily life.

  • National E10 — World's basic human rights & moral responsibilities
  • National E11 — Experience the internationally disadvantaged
  • National E12 — Observe global issues and plan life actions

Core Competencies — 12-Year Basic Education

Our international curriculum aligns with the following national core competencies across three domains.

A — Autonomous Action
A2
Systematic Thinking & Problem Solving

Understand problems, analyse critically, reason with evidence, and reflect in order to effectively handle real-life challenges.

A3
Planning, Execution & Innovation

Plan and execute projects, explore diverse knowledge, enrich life experience, and innovate to adapt to social change.

B — Communication & Interaction
B1
Symbol Use & Communication Expression

Use language, text, mathematics, physical expression and art to communicate, and empathise with others in daily life.

B2
Technology, Information & Media Literacy

Use technology and media effectively, cultivate related ethics and media literacy, and critically analyse information.

C — Social Participation
C2
Interpersonal Relationships & Teamwork

Build positive relationships, communicate and coordinate with others, include diverse perspectives, and participate actively in community and service.

C3
Multiculturalism & International Understanding

Identify with one's own culture while respecting and appreciating others, care about global issues, and develop an open, peaceful worldview.

Science Programme

Hands-On Science & Maker Education

A dedicated specialist science course for Grades 3–6, integrating Astronomy, Geology, Biology, Physics, Chemistry and maker technology. Students learn by doing — building, testing, and presenting their creations.

About the Programme

Starting from students' own life experiences, the Science Programme combines popular science knowledge across disciplines with hands-on maker projects. Students practice, problem-solve, and learn to share their creative process with others.

The curriculum also integrates the Base School Project Plan, with dedicated thematic research projects each year that change by grade level.

4 Grade Levels
2 Semesters
5 STEM Disciplines
4 Maker Projects

Maker Projects by Grade

Each grade level has a dedicated Base School Project that runs across both semesters, building progressively in complexity.

Grade 3

Strandbeest

"The New Species" — Modern Da Vinci Challenge

Semester 1

Understand Strandbeest principles and mechanical design. Test knowledge of physics and mechanics. Explore how to "bring a bionic beast to life."

Semester 2

Study wind-powered motion principles. Use tablets, LEGO bricks and programming to build creatures that avoid obstacles. Integrate crafts, technology and art.

Science Experiments include:

Origami flowers · Air cannon · Paper ball elevator · Magnetic slime · Invisible ink · Origami cranes that move · Dancing paper cup figures

Grade 4

Solar Panels

Renewable Energy & SDG 7 — Affordable Clean Energy

Semester 1

Explore solar energy applications & benefits. Connect to SDG 7 (Clean Energy). Define renewable energy. Observe various "power vehicles" and understand basic design principles.

Semester 2

Collect and organise renewable energy data. Make charts and compare. Build power vehicles hands-on — rubber-band cars, solar cars, battery push cars.

Science Experiments include:

Surface tension · Lava lamp · Floating ship · Periscope DIY · Catapult · Rubber-band aircraft · Rubber-band power car · Parachute · Magnetic levitation

Grade 5

T-Robot

Coding & Robotics — From Basics to Life Missions

Semester 1

Learn basic T-Robot command cards. Plan routes and navigate obstacles. Complete beginner life-task design challenges.

Semester 2

Review T-Robot operations. Design and complete advanced life-task challenges using programming logic and creative problem-solving.

Grade 6

Technology Sensor Light

Electronics Design — Maker Case Circuit & Auto Switch

Semester 1

Design principles — power on and off. Design the Maker Case housing structure and circuit layout.

Semester 2

Build the rolling ball automatic switch mechanism. Design and assemble the outer casing. Complete and demonstrate the finished sensor light.

Science Programme Objectives & Competencies

Course Objectives

  • Learn by doing — hands-on practice and problem-solving
  • Self-discover through exploration and observation
  • Share the creative process with peers and the community
  • Integrate with the Base School Project Plan each year
