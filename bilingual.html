Bilingual education

From Grade 1 to Grade 6, English is not just a subject at Qing Pu — it is a language our students use to think, ask, discover, and create.

🇹🇼

Taiwan's 2030 Bilingual Nation policy

In 2018, Taiwan set a national goal: by 2030, government services and public schools would operate bilingually in Mandarin and English. Qing Pu is one of Taoyuan's leading schools putting that vision into practice through the EMI (English as a Medium of Instruction) model — with dedicated bilingual teachers, English-medium subject lessons, and a school culture that treats English as a living language, not a test subject. Our bilingual programme is now in its fifth year, and enrollment has grown from 450 to 1,800 students since it began.

📚 In the classroom

Subject overviews

A closer look at what each bilingual subject involves.

🌍
Gr. 1–2

Life Studies

生活課程（雙語）

Young learners explore their immediate world — family, nature, seasons, and community — using both English and Mandarin. Lessons are activity-based, combining songs, crafts, outdoor observation, and simple science experiments to make language learning tangible from the very first year.

  • Hands-on classroom activities
  • Nature observation journaling
  • Songs and chants in English
  • Seasonal and cultural themes
🤝
Gr. 3–6

All Together

綜合活動（雙語）

Integrated activity lessons connect English to real-world community situations. Students learn to navigate everyday contexts in English — from introducing themselves to discussing local landmarks — while developing collaborative skills through group projects and presentations. Local bilingual teachers take on more of this subject as students move into Grade 5 and 6.

  • Community-based projects
  • Group presentations in English
  • Field trip preparation & reflection
  • Cross-cultural comparison activities
🏃
Gr. 3–6

PE & Art

體育．藝術（雙語）

These specialist classes are taught by bilingual teachers using English instructions, vocabulary, and feedback throughout — PE games and warm-ups, and art critique, so English becomes natural in movement and making, not just in the classroom.

  • English instructions during PE games
  • Art vocabulary and critique in English
  • Bilingual sports day emceeing
  • Cross-subject creative projects
🌐
Gr. 1–6

International Education

國際教育（青埔特色課程）

International Education is Qing Pu's own distinctive curriculum — developed in-house and not a standard Ministry of Education subject. It broadens students' awareness of the world beyond Taiwan through topics including global cultures, sustainable development, human rights, and peace — all discussed bilingually to give students the language and perspective to engage with an interconnected world.

  • Qingpu-developed curriculum
  • Global culture comparisons
  • Sustainable development themes
  • Cross-school exchange activities

💡 How we teach

Our bilingual approach

We follow a CLIL-inspired model — Content and Language Integrated Learning — where English is the vehicle, not the destination.

01

Language as a tool, not a subject

Rather than teaching English in isolation, our teachers use it as the medium to teach Life Studies, PE, Art, and integrated activities. Students absorb vocabulary and grammar through doing real things — not drilling grammar rules.

02

Code-switching is welcome

We do not require students to speak only English. Moving between Mandarin and English naturally — the way bilingual people do in real life — reduces anxiety and keeps focus on understanding, not performance.

03

Bilingual specialist teachers

Our local bilingual teachers are all graduates of English-related university departments, teaching subjects like PE, Art, and International Education in English. Foreign teachers also take part in monthly seminars, regular observation classes, and the school's English festival activities and competitions.

04

Real-world English contexts

Lessons reference our neighbourhood. The THSR station five minutes away, the airport MRT, the nearby Museum of Art — these become content for English discussion, giving students immediate, local reasons to use the language.

05

Digital exchange with partner schools

Students are grouped and take turns video-calling international partner schools each semester on a designated topic — a chance to use English with real peers abroad, not just classmates and teachers.

06

STREAM thinking, evaluated for speaking

We aim for the STREAM educational spirit — Science, Technology, Reading, Engineering, Arts, and Maths — combined with digital tools. Bilingual assessment prioritizes listening and speaking ability through varied, multi-format evaluation, supplemented by paper tests rather than led by them.
