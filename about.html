Our story

Eight decades of history

Qing Pu grew from a one-room branch school into a landmark campus that anchors the Qingpu new district.

1946

Founded as a branch school

Planning began in August 1946, and the Qingpu Branch of Xinjie Public School was officially established that December — serving the growing farming community in the Qingpu area of Taoyuan with a simple wooden structure and only a handful of classrooms.

1950

Became an independent school

After several years as a branch, the school was officially established as an independent elementary school, serving students from across the Qingpu community with its own dedicated staff and facilities.

1968

Renamed Qing Pu Elementary School

The school was officially renamed 青埔國民小學 (Qing Pu National Elementary School), taking its identity from the Qingpu neighbourhood — a name meaning "green and fertile land."

2015 – 2018

New campus — the paper plane is born

Construction began in 2015, with the new building entering use in 2016 and the project reaching completion in 2018 — designed around the concept of a paper plane in flight. The architecture features folded roof lines, four identity colors drawn from the surrounding transport landscape, and open spaces designed to inspire curiosity and movement. New buildings and playground renovations continue to this day.

New building Award-winning design
2018

Bilingual education begins

Alongside the new campus, Qing Pu launched its bilingual program under the EMI (English as a Medium of Instruction) model. Together with the area's rapid urban development, enrollment grew from around 450 students to roughly 1,800 — and the school became known as much for bilingual education as for its distinctive architecture.

EMI model 450 → 1,800 students
Today

A bilingual school for the 21st century

Now part of Taiwan's 2030 Bilingual Nation initiative, Qing Pu integrates English across the curriculum from Grade 1 through Grade 6. Situated steps from THSR Taoyuan Station, our students are connected to the world — and learning the language to navigate it — every single day.

What we stand for

Our school vision

Three words guide everything we do — every classroom, every activity, every year.

Hope

😊

School should be a place children want to be. We design our days — lessons, play, relationships — so that joy is not a reward at the end of learning, but woven through it.

Every child has something they are good at. Our job is to find it, celebrate it, and use it as a foundation for growing in every other area — including a second language.

Character at the core

Alongside academics, Qing Pu places deep emphasis on character education — nurturing students to embody gratitude, respect, care, and responsibility through daily school life, curriculum, and community activities. A good person and a capable learner are, for us, the same goal.

Architecture

A school built like a paper plane

The 2015 – 2018 campus redesign was guided by a single concept: a paper plane lifting off the ground. Every design decision connects back to that image.

The paper plane concept

The roofline of the main building traces the silhouette of a paper plane mid-flight — rising from the entrance, peaking at the central hall, and sweeping back down at the rear. Seen from above, the folded forms echo the creases in a sheet of paper just thrown into the air.

Paper planes are the universal symbol of childhood imagination — simple, handmade, and capable of travelling further than you expect. That is exactly what we want for our students.

The theme carries through to the school gate, where a public art installation — "Paper Plane Carrying Dreams" — introduced our two campus mascots, Xiao Qing and A Pu, who greet students every morning.

Four identity colors

The building's color palette was drawn directly from the transport landmarks that surround the school — making Qing Pu visually inseparable from its place. Each color is even assigned to its own floor, rising from the ground up.

Qingpu green — 1F The fertile farmland the school's name comes from
THSR orange — 2F Taiwan High Speed Rail Taoyuan Station
Aviation blue — 3F The sky above Taoyuan International Airport
MRT purple — 4F Taoyuan Airport MRT, line A18

Beyond academics

Student life

Qing Pu is known for two things beyond the classroom — competitive athletics and a thriving music programme.

🏃

Track & field

Our track and field team trains regularly under dedicated coaches and consistently achieves strong results in inter-school competitions — one of the things Qing Pu is best known for, alongside our bilingual programme.

🎻

Music club

Running since 2023, our music club brings together wind and string instruments under the guidance of professional teachers, with students regularly performing and competing to excellent results.

🎯 What we offer

School programmes

Beyond the national curriculum, Qing Pu has developed distinctive programmes rooted in the school's values and local identity.

📚

Reading literacy

We promote reading strategies and diverse reading activities across all grade levels, fostering a love for reading and building strong comprehension habits from an early age. The school library and regular silent reading time are central to daily school life.

🏘️

Local cultural exploration

We integrate Qingpu's local character and resources into our curriculum — helping students understand their hometown, its rapid development, and its connections to the wider world. Students build a sense of local identity alongside a global perspective.

🎭

Specialty clubs

A wide range of after-school clubs — arts, sports, science, and information technology — gives every student the chance to discover and develop interests beyond the regular timetable. Club activities are open to all grades.

💻

Technology-integrated learning

Interactive whiteboards, tablets, and digital tools are embedded into everyday teaching across subjects. Students develop information literacy and computational thinking as a natural part of their school day, not as a separate subject.
