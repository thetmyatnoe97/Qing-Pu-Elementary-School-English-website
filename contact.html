❓ Common questions

Frequently asked questions

For families and visitors contacting us for the first time.

Yes — we welcome enquiries in English. The bilingual programme coordinator and several teachers are comfortable with English correspondence. We aim to reply to all English emails within two school days.

Enrolment is managed through Taoyuan City's centralised school assignment system. Students are assigned to a school based on their registered household address (戶籍). If you live in the Qingpu catchment area, your child will automatically be assigned to our school. For detailed guidance, please contact the Academic Affairs office (Ext. 200) or visit the Taoyuan City Education Bureau.

Yes — we welcome visits from prospective families, researchers, and educators. Please contact us at least one week in advance using the form above or by calling the main office. Campus tours are available Monday to Friday between 09:00 and 16:00 during the school year.

Yes — children with valid residency in Taiwan (ARC holders, APRC holders, and dependent family members of foreign nationals) can attend public schools including Qing Pu. The same catchment-area rules apply. Contact the Academic Affairs office for the specific documents required for non-citizen enrolment.

Please use the contact form and select "Media / research request" as the subject. Include your name, organisation, and the nature of your request. All media and academic enquiries are reviewed by the principal's office. We ask that on-campus photography and filming be pre-approved in writing.

We follow a CLIL (Content and Language Integrated Learning) approach rather than full immersion. Core subjects like Life Studies, Science, and integrated activities are taught bilingually — lessons use English as the primary medium but teachers support comprehension in Mandarin when needed. This reduces anxiety and produces stronger long-term language outcomes than strict immersion for primary-age children. Visit our Bilingual education page for more detail.
